# ClauseGuard — Outreach Kit

A starting toolkit for landing your first audit clients. Edit freely —
swap in real numbers once you have your first case study, and personalize
every `[bracket]` before sending.

---

## 1. The pitch, in one sentence

> "We review your vendor contracts against what you're actually being
> billed, find overcharges and missed discounts, and you only pay us if
> we find money — typically 15-30% of your annual spend on that vendor."

---

## 2. Cold LinkedIn DM (to Controllers, VPs Finance, Office Managers)

**Subject / opener:**

> Hi [Name] — quick one. Most companies are quietly overpaying vendors
> because invoices drift from contract terms over time (price caps
> exceeded, discounts that stop applying, fees that get billed twice).
>
> I run a quick AI-powered audit that compares a vendor contract against
> its invoices and flags exactly where this is happening — with the
> contract clause and invoice line that prove it.
>
> Want me to run it on one vendor contract for free? Takes 5 minutes of
> your time (just need the contract + a couple recent invoices), and
> there's no cost unless we find recoverable money.

**Follow-up if no response after 4-5 days:**

> Following up on this — happy to just send you a sample report from a
> similar company so you can see the format before deciding. No pressure
> either way.

## 3. Cold email (to CFOs / Controllers)

**Subject line options:**
- "Free audit: are you overpaying [Vendor Type] vendors?"
- "Found $[X]/yr in vendor overcharges for [similar company] — want the same check?"
- "5-minute ask: contract vs. invoice audit"

**Body:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Vendor contracts almost always drift from what's actually billed over
> time — rate increases that exceed the contract's escalation cap,
> discounts that quietly stop applying, one-time setup fees that get
> billed again. Most finance teams don't have time to cross-check every
> invoice against the original contract terms.
>
> I built a tool that does exactly that: it reads a vendor contract
> (plus any amendments/MOUs) and your invoices from that vendor, and
> produces an audit report flagging every discrepancy — with the dollar
> impact, the exact contract clause, and the invoice line that proves it.
>
> I'd like to run it on one of your vendor contracts at no cost. If we
> find recoverable money, we work on a contingency basis (you keep the
> majority of whatever's recovered). If we find nothing, you've lost
> nothing.
>
> Would you be open to sending over one contract + 2-3 recent invoices
> from the same vendor? Happy to sign an NDA first if that's easier.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
> [Phone / calendar link]

## 4. CPA / bookkeeping firm partnership pitch

CPAs and bookkeepers already have client contracts and invoices on file
— they're the highest-leverage channel because one relationship = many
client introductions.

> Hi [Name],
>
> I work with companies to audit vendor contracts against their billing
> — finding overcharges, missed discounts, and contract terms that
> expired without anyone noticing (which often means a client is still
> being billed under old terms they no longer have to honor).
>
> Since you already have visibility into your clients' vendor spend, I'd
> love to offer this as a value-add you can bring to them — a free audit
> on one vendor contract, no cost unless we find money, and a referral
> fee back to you on anything recovered.
>
> Would you be open to a quick call to see if this fits a few of your
> clients?

## 5. Free-audit intake checklist (send to a prospect who says yes)

To run the audit, I'll need:

- [ ] The vendor's master contract (PDF or scanned copy is fine)
- [ ] Any amendments, MOUs, side letters, or renewal notices for that vendor
- [ ] 1-3 recent invoices from that same vendor (PDF, or a CSV export from your accounting system)

Turnaround: same day. You'll receive a PDF audit report with every
finding, the contract clause behind it, and — if the contract has
lapsed without a new one signed — a section on where that leaves you
legally and how to move forward.

## 6. Objection handling

**"We already have a procurement/finance team that checks this."**
> Totally fair — most teams check pricing at signing, but very few
> re-check it every month against live invoices. That drift is exactly
> what this catches, and it costs you nothing to find out if there's
> anything there.

**"How do I know this is legitimate / not a scam?"**
> Happy to start with a sample report on a contract you don't mind
> sharing, or sign an NDA first. The output is fully transparent — every
> finding cites the exact contract clause and invoice line, so you can
> verify it yourself before acting on anything.

**"What's the catch with the contingency fee?"**
> No catch — if we don't find anything recoverable, you pay nothing.
> The fee only applies to money you weren't going to recover otherwise.

## 7. Where to find prospects (priority order)

1. **CPA/bookkeeping firms** in your area — search LinkedIn for
   "bookkeeping" + your city, or use your own accountant as a first
   intro.
2. **LinkedIn search**: "Controller" or "VP Finance" + industries with
   contract-heavy vendor relationships (property management, healthcare
   practices, manufacturing, logistics, multi-location retail/restaurant
   groups).
3. **Local Chamber of Commerce / business networking groups** — these
   are warm, low-competition rooms full of small-business owners who
   sign vendor contracts themselves.
4. **Your own network** — anyone running a business with software
   subscriptions, telecom, insurance, or equipment leases is a candidate
   for a free first audit.
